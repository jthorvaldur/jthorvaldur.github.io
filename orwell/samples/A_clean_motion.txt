RESPONDENT'S MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY

The Respondent moves the Court to compel the Petitioner to produce the following documents within fourteen days:

1. Bank statements for accounts ending 1111, 2222, and 3333 from January 1, 2023 through December 31, 2024.
2. Brokerage statements for the brokerage account ending 4444 for the same period.
3. The 2023 and 2024 federal tax returns with all schedules and attachments.

The Petitioner served Discovery Requests on March 12, 2025. The Respondent served full responses on April 15, 2025. The Respondent served reciprocal Discovery Requests on April 18, 2025. As of June 5, 2025, the Petitioner has produced 4 of 17 requested documents.

The Respondent has sent three follow-up emails dated May 2, May 18, and May 30. No documents were produced in response. The Petitioner's counsel responded on May 31 stating that production would occur "in due course" without specifying a date.

Under the applicable rules, a party is entitled to discovery of all matters relevant to the case. The 13 outstanding requests go to income, asset value, and dissipation — the core issues set for trial on July 22, 2025.

The Respondent requests an order compelling production within fourteen days and awarding fees of $1,200 for the time spent on follow-up correspondence.
