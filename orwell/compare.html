What changed — three causes, only one is "wrong factors"

Cause 1 · calibration

v1 ran a wider calibration (not "fake")

0.85 vs 0.37

v1 sat opposing docs at 0.58–0.85 and ours near 0.05. Re-run today the scorer still separates clean from boilerplate (proven on Axis Breakdown) — but on this corpus it floors clean docs ~0.15 and tops real docs at 0.37, a narrower spread. The v1 extremes were a more aggressive calibration, not a scorer that can't tell the sides apart.

Cause 2 · the cohorts

The contrast group was never captured

5 opposing vs 119 neutral

v1's high band was 18 docs (counsel + agency). v2's opposing cohort (B) is only 5; the build_corpus.py regex routes anything without counsel-name tokens to the default cohort X (119). Opposing filings were almost certainly absorbed into "neutral" — and 4 court orders leaked into "us."

Cause 3 · the factors

The weighted axes don't discriminate

1 dead · several fire on all

One axis (Authority Laundering) fires in 1/300 docs; several (Exemption Stacking, Referral Loops, Hedge, Temporal) sit near zero. The axes that do move (Stated-vs-Actual, Nominalization, Sentence Burial) are length/style features that fire on everyone, us included — raising the floor without separating sides. So yes: partly mis-weighted factors.

Verdict. The scorer is fine and does separate clean from doublespeak — proven by re-scoring labeled samples on the Axis Breakdown page, and the composite is exactly Σ(axis × v1-weight). The flat dashboard is overwhelmingly a cohort-labeling problem: the highest-scoring real docs sit in X and A, while the opposing cohort B captured only 5. Secondary: a couple of axes barely fire. To re-establish a fair contrast, fix cohort capture first (re-classify B from real opposing-counsel paths, pull court orders out of A), then recalibrate the flat axes. The v1 0.05/0.85 extremes were a wider/older calibration — don't treat them as ground truth.
