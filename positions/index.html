Mathematical Framework

Definitions

Let T = {shijūhatte, kāmasūtra, fángzhōngshù, perfumed garden, greco-roman}
Let P = ⋃ Pt be the universal position set, |P| = 133
Let M = {facing, rear, sitting, standing, side, inverted, oral, special}
Let μ: P → M assign each position its mechanical category

Similarity metric
σ: P × P → [0,1] where σ(p,q) estimates mechanical equivalence
σ(p,q) = 1 iff p and q are mechanically identical
d(p,q) = 1 - σ(p,q) is a dissimilarity (pseudo-metric)

Equivalence relation
For threshold ε ∈ [0,1]: p ~ε q ⇔ σ(p,q) ≥ ε
Equivalence classes [p]ε partition the mapped subset of P
At ε = 0.75: 25 classes identified

Tradition vectors
v: T → ℤ&sup8; maps each tradition to its category distribution
Cosine similarity cos(v(t₁), v(t₂)) measures structural affinity
Jaccard index J(A,B) = |A ∩ B| / |A ∪ B| over occupied categories

Adjacency matrix
