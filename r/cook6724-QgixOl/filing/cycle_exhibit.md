# EXHIBIT — ADVERSARIAL PROCESS CYCLE ANALYSIS

**Case:** In re Marriage of Thorarinson v. Atagan, No. 2024 D 6724
**Prepared:** April 28, 2026
**Purpose:** Structural analysis of self-defeating cycles in the current adversarial process that prevent resolution and harm the minor children's welfare.

---

## Cycle 1: Contempt-Nondisclosure Loop [CRITICAL]

**Duration:** 345 days and counting

**Description:** Respondent files contempt petition alleging non-payment. Petitioner documents $366,929 in payments. Respondent credits $44,641 because she does not disclose the accounts that received the remaining funds. Petitioner cannot purge contempt without accounting for funds in undisclosed accounts. Respondent refuses to disclose accounts. Cycle repeats with each new contempt petition.

**Sequence:**

1. [2025-05-19] Respondent: Contempt alleged: Joel didn't pay $10K/month →
2. [2026-04-17] Petitioner: Joel documents $366,929 in payments (Exhibit D) →
3. [2026-04-28] Respondent: Heather credits only $44,641 — rest went to undisclosed accounts →
4. [2026-04-17] Petitioner: Joel requests disclosure of accounts x9056, x2573 + others →
5. [2026-03-30] Respondent: Heather refuses to respond to Rule 214 (28+ days overdue) →
6. [2026-04-17] Respondent: Without disclosure, payment amount remains disputed → new contempt petition → *(returns to step 1)*

**Impact on Minor Children:** The dispute over payment amounts prevents resolution of the financial case. Meanwhile, the mortgage on the children's home is delinquent. No court order can correctly allocate resources when one party's financial picture is 80% hidden.

**Resolution:** Court orders complete financial disclosure from Respondent within 14 days (Motion to Compel).

## Cycle 2: Body Attachment Paradox [HIGH]

**Duration:** 67 days and counting

**Description:** Court warns body attachment if Petitioner doesn't appear in person. Petitioner is out of state with $0 income since December 2025. Petitioner cannot afford travel to Chicago. Petitioner's inability to pay is the subject of the contempt, creating a circular trap: cannot purge contempt without appearing, cannot appear without resources, cannot earn resources while under threat of arrest.

**Sequence:**

1. [2026-02-20] Court: Court orders in-person appearance May 5 →
2. [2025-12-05] Petitioner: Joel terminated from Eisler, $0 income →
3. [2026-04-28] Petitioner: Joel cannot afford travel to Chicago →
4. [2026-02-20] Court: Body attachment warning if failure to appear →
5. [2026-04-28] Petitioner: Arrest risk deters employment prospects → *(returns to step 1)*

**Impact on Minor Children:** If Petitioner is arrested, he cannot earn income to pay support or mortgage. The children's father becomes incarcerated for inability to pay, not unwillingness. No financial resolution is possible while the primary earner is in custody.

**Resolution:** Court allows Zoom appearance or modifies support to $0 income level (Motion to Modify).

## Cycle 3: Employment-Support Destruction Cycle [CRITICAL]

**Duration:** 366 days and counting

**Description:** Respondent's counsel served subpoena at Petitioner's workplace (Eisler Capital, London) on April 28, 2025. This contributed to garden leave (August 2025) and termination (December 2025). Petitioner's income dropped from $250,000/year to $0. Respondent then filed contempt for non-payment of $10,000/month support set when income was $250,000+. The action that destroyed the income source is now the basis for contempt for not having income.

**Sequence:**

1. [2025-04-30] Court: Support set at $10K/month based on $250K+ income →
2. [2025-04-28] Respondent: Heather's counsel subpoenas Joel at workplace (Eisler, London) →
3. [2025-08-05] Petitioner: Joel placed on garden leave →
4. [2025-12-05] Petitioner: Joel terminated — income drops to $0 →
5. [2025-10-28] Respondent: Heather files contempt for non-payment of $10K/month →
6. [2026-04-20] Petitioner: Joel seeks to modify support to reflect $0 income →
7. [2026-04-28] Respondent: Heather opposes modification, calls unemployment 'voluntary' → *(returns to step 1)*

**Impact on Minor Children:** The children's primary financial support was destroyed by litigation action. The subpoena at the workplace contributed to job loss. The resulting $0 income means no support, no mortgage, and potential foreclosure of the family home.

**Resolution:** Court modifies support to reflect current $0 income (Motion to Modify) and acknowledges workplace subpoena as contributing factor.

## Cycle 4: Foreclosure Deadlock — Children's Home at Risk [CRITICAL]

**Duration:** 67 days and counting

**Description:** The marital residence at 430 Wilshire Dr, Wilmette (where two minor children reside) has a delinquent mortgage (~$4,392/month). Petitioner has $0 income and cannot pay. Respondent claims $0 income despite controlling $290,000+ in undisclosed assets. Petitioner filed Motion to Sell — Respondent opposes. Court ordered mortgage current by March 22 — neither party complied. The home approaches foreclosure while the parties litigate over who should pay from assets that haven't been disclosed.

**Sequence:**

1. [2026-02-20] : Mortgage delinquent on children's home →
2. [2025-12-05] Petitioner: Joel has $0 income, cannot pay →
3. [2025-05-16] Respondent: Heather claims $0 income but controls $290K+ undisclosed →
4. [2025-11-13] Petitioner: Joel files Motion to Sell — Heather opposes →
5. [2026-02-20] Court: Court orders mortgage current by March 22 — not complied →
6. [2026-04-28] : Home approaches foreclosure — children lose residence → *(returns to step 1)*

**Impact on Minor Children:** Ariana (16) and Roman (13) live in this home. If foreclosed, they lose their residence, school district (New Trier), and stability. Neither party is paying the mortgage because the financial picture is unresolved. The financial picture is unresolved because Respondent will not disclose her accounts.

**Resolution:** Court compels full financial disclosure, determines available assets, and orders mortgage payment from identified funds or sale of residence.

## Summary

This analysis identifies 4 adversarial cycles, 3 of which are critical.
Each cycle shares a common resolution: **complete financial disclosure by both parties.**
Without disclosure, the Court cannot determine correct support amounts, cannot resolve contempt allegations, cannot address the mortgage crisis, and cannot protect the children's housing stability.

Petitioner has filed a Motion to Compel Complete Financial Disclosure (April 20, 2026). Respondent's Rule 214 production is 29 days overdue with zero response. Three meet-and-confer letters have received zero substantive replies.
