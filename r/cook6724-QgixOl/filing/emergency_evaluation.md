# Emergency Motion Evaluation

**Scenario:** Tarara Admission of Undisclosed Accounts (April 28, 2026)
**Evaluated:** 2026-04-28
**Overall Score:** 0.73 / 1.00
**Meets Standard:** YES

---

## Prong: SUDDEN — MET (80%)

Tarara's Reply to the Third Contempt Petition, filed April 28, 2026, admitted for the first time that Chase accounts x9056 (Heather's business checking since November 2024) and x2573 (Heather's personal checking since 2019) exist. Prior to this filing, Heather had never acknowledged these accounts in any court document. This is a NEW admission that occurred on a specific date. The FDA filed May 16, 2025 omits both accounts. The admission transforms the FDA from 'arguably incomplete' to 'provably false' under 735 ILCS 5/1-109.

**Evidence:**
- H_REPLY_PICC3: 'Chase Account x9056 is the checking account associated with HEATHER's business'
- H_REPLY_PICC3: 'Chase Account x2573 is HEATHER's personal checking account that has existed since 2019'
- H_FDA (May 16, 2025): neither x9056 nor x2573 listed
- 735 ILCS 5/1-109: verification under penalties of perjury

---

## Prong: UNFORESEEN — MET (65%)

Joel suspected these accounts existed based on bank transfer records, which is why he filed Exhibit E identifying them. However, suspecting an account exists is different from opposing counsel ADMITTING it exists while the FDA omits it. The admission itself — counsel confirming the FDA is incomplete under oath — could not have been predicted. However, a judge might argue that Joel knew about these accounts and should have raised this earlier, weakening the 'unforeseen' prong.

**Evidence:**
- J_EX_E: Joel identified x9056 and x2573 in April 17, 2026 filing
- H_REPLY_PICC3: Tarara confirmed both accounts April 28
- The ADMISSION is new; the SUSPICION was not

---

## Prong: HARM — Damage to Property — MET (75%)

Two forms of property damage: (1) The marital residence at 430 Wilshire Dr, Wilmette is delinquent on mortgage (~$4,392/month). Two minor children reside there. Foreclosure risk is ongoing and worsening daily. (2) A body attachment/contempt finding based on a provably false FDA constitutes irreparable harm to Petitioner's liberty and ability to earn income. The GAO standard explicitly includes 'damage to property' — the home qualifies. However, the covering judge already considered the foreclosure argument on April 28 and denied it.

**Evidence:**
- Mortgage delinquent since ~Dec 2025 (Joel $0 income, Heather $0 disclosed)
- Monthly payment: $4,392
- Children reside at property (Ariana 16, Roman 13, New Trier school district)
- Feb 20 order required mortgage current by Mar 22 — not complied

---

## Strategic Risk Assessment

HIGH. Judge Rosen denied the emergency motion on April 28 — the same day. Filing another emergency within days carries these risks:
1. Confirms Tarara's 'frivolous filings' narrative
2. A second denial on the same week damages credibility with Jannusch
3. Jannusch is out this week — another covering judge would also be unfamiliar
4. May 5 hearing is 7 days away — the 'wait for the scheduled hearing' argument gets stronger
5. Two emergency denials in one week could be raised by Tarara to argue sanctions

---

## Recommendation

DO NOT file a new emergency motion this week.

The three-prong test is arguably met (score: 0.73), but the strategic risk far outweighs the benefit. The Tarara admission is POWERFUL — but its power is maximized when presented to Jannusch on May 5 as part of the Motion to Strike, not burned on another emergency that a covering judge denies.

RECOMMENDED INSTEAD:
1. File a Supplemental Brief incorporating the April 28 admission as new evidence supporting the Motion to Strike FDA — this gets it before Jannusch without the emergency standard hurdle
2. File the brief as 'Notice of Supplemental Authority' or 'Supplemental Brief in Support of Pending Motions' — standard practice, no emergency needed
3. Lead with this on May 5: 'Your Honor, since our last filing, Respondent's counsel admitted under oath that the two accounts I identified as undisclosed do in fact exist. Neither appears on the sworn Financial Affidavit.'

The admission is a gift. Don't waste it on another emergency. Use it on May 5 where Jannusch — who knows this case — can act on it.
