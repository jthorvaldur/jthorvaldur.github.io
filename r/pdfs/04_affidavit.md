My employment contract with Eisler Capital (UK) Ltd, dated 17 May 2024, provides at clause 6.1 a base salary of **$250,000** per annum. These were non-recurring. They were not clawed back because my termination was employer-initiated, not voluntary resignation or misconduct. Even adding base + sign-on + guarantee, the first-year gross total was approximately $1.25 million — before United Kingdom taxes of approximately 52%. My recurring annual compensation without trading profits was the base salary alone: $250,000 gross, approximately $120,000 net after UK taxes. My employment was terminated by the employer on 2025-12-05, and I have had **$0 in earned income** since that date. The $1,200,000 figure has been cited repeatedly in opposing filings and does not reflect my actual contractual compensation.

   (c) The same filing states at paragraph 27 that Heather Kim Atagan 'has not been employed outside of the home for the duration of the marriage.' Heather Kim Atagan operates Atagan Music Studios, and her own financial affidavit reports $2,000 in gross business receipts for the current year.

   These inaccuracies are not minor. The $1,200,000 figure has been cited repeatedly in opposing filings as the basis for support calculations. The actual figure — after the taxes that the United Kingdom requires — is less than half that amount, and is now $0.
