### IV. DEFENSE: INABILITY TO COMPLY, NOT WILLFUL REFUSAL

11. Indirect civil contempt requires proof that the alleged contemnor had the
ability to comply with the court's order and willfully refused to do so. See
*In re Marriage of Sharp*, 2014 IL App (2d) 130547, ¶ 40; *City of Chicago v.
Le Mirage, Inc.*, 2013 IL App (1st) 113645, ¶ 20.

12. Joel Thorarinson did not willfully refuse to comply with this Court's
orders. To the contrary, Joel Thorarinson has made substantial payments and
transfers during the period covered by the contempt petitions, as set forth
below.

13. **Verified payments:** Joel Thorarinson has made documented payments
totaling **$256,888.07** across the following categories:

   - Child Expense: $6,985.00
   - Family Support: $27,435.12
   - Medical: $222,467.95

   These payments are set forth in detail in Exhibit D.

14. **Direct transfers to Heather Kim Atagan's accounts:** In addition to the
payments listed above, Joel Thorarinson made **13
transfers totaling $110,041.00** from the joint Chase account
ending in ...2151 to two Chase accounts ending in ...9056 and ...2573. These
transfers were made to support Heather Kim Atagan's financial needs, including
the development of her business, Atagan Music Studios. Joel Thorarinson made
these transfers in good faith to help Heather Kim Atagan build financial
independence. These accounts have not been disclosed by Heather Kim Atagan on
her financial affidavit.

   - Account ...2573: $48,041.00
   - Account ...9056: $62,000.00

   These transfers are detailed in Exhibit E and verified against bank CSV records.

15. **Combined total:** Taking verified payments and direct transfers together,
Joel Thorarinson has provided or transferred **$366,929.07** during
the period at issue. This is not the conduct of a party willfully refusing to
comply with support obligations.

16. **Income termination:** Joel Thorarinson's employment with Eisler Capital
was terminated by the employer effective 2025-12-05.
The termination was employer-initiated and was not the result of voluntary
resignation or misconduct. Joel Thorarinson has had **$0 earned income** since
that date. His present inability to meet the full $10,000/month obligation is
the result of changed circumstances, not willful refusal.
