## RESPONSE TO VERIFIED PETITIONS
FOR INDIRECT CIVIL CONTEMPT

NOW COMES the Petitioner, Joel Thorarinson, appearing pro se, and
respectfully files this Response to the First Verified Petition for Indirect
Civil Contempt, filed 2025-05-19, and the
Second Verified Petition for Indirect Civil Contempt, filed
2025-10-28, pursuant to the Rule to Show
Cause issued in this Court's Order of 2026-02-20, and
states as follows:

1. On 2026-02-20, this Court entered an Order granting
in part the Emergency Motion, establishing a mortgage arrearage of
$13,495.94, requiring Petitioner to
bring the mortgage current by 2026-03-22,
issuing a Rule to Show Cause on both contempt petitions, and setting this matter
for hearing on 2026-05-05 at
1:30 PM.

2. Petitioner acknowledges the Court's Order, the Rule to Show Cause, and the
Court's authority in this matter. Petitioner does not seek to evade this
proceeding and submits this Response to address the allegations before the Court.

3. Petitioner respectfully requests leave to file this Response instanter.
Petitioner's response was due 2026-03-20.
During the relevant period, Petitioner's prior counsel filed a Motion to
Withdraw on 2026-04-01, leaving Petitioner without
representation. Petitioner is pro se, resides in Canada, and was experiencing
medical and treatment-related limitations that impaired his ability to manage
litigation and financial matters. The late filing was not willful. Petitioner
asks the Court to accept this Response in the interest of deciding the matter
on a complete record.

4. Petitioner acknowledges that the mortgage was not brought current by
2026-03-22 as ordered. Petitioner
states that his failure to cure the arrearage within that time was not willful
refusal, but occurred during the same period of transition and disrupted
financial circumstances described above.

5. As to the allegations in the First Verified Petition:

   a. With respect to communications outside OurFamilyWizard, Petitioner
   acknowledges that on May 11, 2025, he sent text messages instead of
   communicating through OurFamilyWizard. This occurred during a period of
   acute personal distress and was not intended as a deliberate violation.

   b. With respect to the allegations arising from the May 11, 2025 incident
   involving access to Heather Kim Atagan's email account, Petitioner does not
   dispute that the conduct occurred. Petitioner acknowledges that the conduct
   was improper.

   c. With respect to the allegation concerning approximately $4,020 in credit
   card payments from the joint account, Petitioner states that those payments
   were made on accounts used for marital and family expenses.

6. As to the allegations in the Second Verified Petition:

   a. Petitioner denies that any nonpayment was the result of willful refusal
   to comply. Petitioner's financial circumstances changed materially, as set
   forth below.

   b. With respect to the allegations of improper transactions from marital
   accounts, Petitioner states that many of the transactions at issue were
   credit card payments for marital or family expenses, or transfers to
   Heather Kim Atagan's accounts to support Heather Kim Atagan's financial needs,
   including the development of her business, Atagan Music Studios.

   c. With respect to the financial affidavit, Petitioner acknowledges delay
   and states that he has filed his financial affidavit twice (October 16, 2025
   and November 18, 2025) and is prepared to supplement.

7. Indirect civil contempt requires proof of a willful failure to comply with
a court order despite the present ability to do so. *In re Marriage of Sharp*,
2014 IL App (2d) 130547, ¶ 40. Petitioner respectfully submits that the facts
here do not support a finding of willful refusal.

8. During the period at issue, Petitioner made documented payments totaling
**$256,888.07**, consisting of:

   - Child Expense: $6,985.00
   - Family Support: $27,435.12
   - Medical: $222,467.95

   These payments are reflected in Exhibit D.

9. In addition, records from the joint Chase account ending in ...2151 reflect
13 transfers totaling
**$110,041.00** to accounts ending in ...9056 and ...2573. These
transfers were made to support Heather Kim Atagan's financial needs. See
Exhibit E.

10. The documented payments and transfers total **$366,929.07**.
These amounts are inconsistent with a claim that Petitioner was simply refusing
to provide financial support.

11. Petitioner's employment with Eisler Capital ended effective
2025-12-05. The separation was employer-initiated
and was not the result of voluntary resignation or misconduct. Since that date,
Petitioner has had **no recurring earned employment income**. His present
inability to satisfy the full support amount is the result of changed financial
circumstances, not willful refusal.

12. Prior pleadings reference income "in excess of $1,200,000" per year.
Petitioner's recurring compensation was not $1,200,000. His employment agreement
provided a base salary of $250,000
per year, along with certain one-time and contingent items. Petitioner has had
$0 earned income since 2025-12-05.

13. The financial record before the Court is incomplete in certain respects
relevant to the contempt allegations. Heather Kim Atagan's financial affidavit,
signed under oath, does not disclose the accounts ending in ...9056 and ...2573,
which received $110,041.00 from the joint account during the
period at issue.

14. The Second Petition's own Exhibit C lists a
$2,000.00 transfer on
2025-05-06 to "Checking Account x9056" as
one of Petitioner's violations — the same account that does not appear in
Heather Kim Atagan's financial affidavit signed ten days earlier.

15. Petitioner does not ask the Court to make adverse findings at this time.
Petitioner seeks leave to supplement the financial record so that the Court may
evaluate the issues on complete information.

16. Petitioner is presently residing in Canada. Petitioner is not evading this
Court and is willing to appear by Zoom or other remote means for the
2026-05-05 hearing or on any other date the Court
directs. Petitioner respectfully requests that the Court decline to issue a body
attachment. Petitioner has filed this Response, is submitting supporting
materials, and is requesting permission to appear remotely.

17. If the Court is inclined to find a basis for contempt, Petitioner
respectfully requests that any purge conditions be limited to conditions within
his present ability to satisfy. Petitioner proposes:

   a. Leave to file corrected and supplemental financial disclosures within
   30 days;

   b. A remote status hearing within 30 days to review compliance;

   c. Such further financial reporting as the Court may reasonably require.

18. With respect to the mortgage arrearage, Petitioner requests that the Court
consider his present lack of recurring earned income, the existing debt burden,
and the need for a realistic compliance schedule. Petitioner's uncle has made
two payments of approximately $4,500 each to prevent foreclosure. A Petition to
Sell the marital residence was filed 2025-11-10;
Heather Kim Atagan opposes the sale.

19. Purge conditions must be achievable. Setting conditions a party cannot meet
converts civil contempt into punishment. *In re Marriage of Logston*, 103 Ill.
2d 266, 285 (1984).

WHEREFORE, Petitioner, Joel Thorarinson, respectfully requests that this Court:

A. Grant leave to file this Response instanter;

B. Deny the First and Second Verified Petitions for Indirect Civil Contempt to
the extent they allege willful refusal to comply;

C. In the alternative, set purge conditions reasonably related to Petitioner's
present ability to comply;

D. Permit Petitioner to appear remotely at the 2026-05-05
hearing;

E. Decline to issue a body attachment;

F. Order Heather Kim Atagan to identify accounts ending in ...9056 and ...2573
and to file an amended financial affidavit;

G. Grant Petitioner leave to file corrected or supplemental financial
disclosures within 30 days;

H. Grant such other and further relief as this Court deems just.
