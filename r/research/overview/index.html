What These Tests Verify

Coherence isn't a domain-specific trick. It's a universal property of system health.

When a jet engine is healthy, its temperature, pressure, and vibration sensors move together in a coherent pattern. When it degrades, those sensors start disagreeing — first subtly, then dramatically. The same behavior appears in power grid voltage traces, heartbeat channels, brain waves, and transaction feature distributions.

These tests verify that coherence collapse is a universal precursor to failure, not an artifact of any single dataset. The equation requires no labeled training data, no per-domain model, and no expert tuning. It works because the physics of breakdown is the same everywhere: systems lose internal agreement before they lose function.

The implication

Any system with multiple correlated signals — a building's electrical load, a fleet of turbines, a patient's vital signs, a microgrid's power flows — can be monitored with one universal metric.

No custom models. No retraining. No data science team. Plug in the sensors, compute Δ, and watch for the drop.
