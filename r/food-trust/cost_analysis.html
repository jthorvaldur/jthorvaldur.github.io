The Stripe Tax on Small Transactions

A $10 jar of honey on Stripe costs $0.59 in fees (5.9%). At commodity scale ($0.25/oz, 12 oz jar = $3), Stripe eats $0.39 = 13%. This is why the exchange uses internal credits for producer-to-producer trades and only touches Stripe for consumer-facing retail or fiat cash-out.

5. Ethereum — When and Why

The exchange ledger is not a blockchain. It's a merkle-proof ledger with UNA governance. But Ethereum adds optional value for:

  • Immutable anchor: Periodically publish ledger root hash to Ethereum ($0.50-5.00/tx on L1, $0.01-0.10 on L2). Proves ledger wasn't tampered with — even if the server burns down.
  • USDC settlement: For producers who want to cash out to stablecoin instead of fiat. L2 fees are negligible.
  • Cross-exchange interop: If multiple regional exchanges exist, Ethereum provides a shared settlement layer. Future concern.

Bottom Line

At 20 batches/month (1,200 lbs, 19,200 oz):

  • DIY testing + sealing: ~$0.014/oz
  • Hosting + L2 blockchain: ~$0.0004/oz
  • Stripe (card) on $50 avg sale: ~$0.091/oz (the expensive part)
  • Total with Stripe: ~$0.10/oz (40% of $0.25 commodity price)
  • Total with internal credits: ~$0.015/oz (6% of commodity price)

The lesson: Stripe eats the margin on small transactions. The exchange should use internal HVC credits for producer-to-producer trades and only touch Stripe for consumer retail and fiat cash-out. That drops the cost stack from 40% to 6%.
