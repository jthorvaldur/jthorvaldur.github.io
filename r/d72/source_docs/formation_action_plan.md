## Critical Assessment of the Structure

### Strengths

- **Wyoming Holdco is the right call.** IP segregation from operating entities is standard best practice. Wyoming's privacy (no public member names), charging order protection, and low annual fees ($60) are real advantages for an IP holding vehicle.
- **Class A enumeration is explicit.** The 10 reserved domains (hiring, budgets, patents, M&A, conversion, etc.) are itemized — far better than implied control and will hold up to scrutiny.
- **Class B exclusions are affirmative.** The operating agreement doesn't just say "advisory only" — it affirmatively states those rights were never granted. That is the correct drafting approach.
- **IP assignment is scheduled, not blanket.** Non-negotiable #3 is honored — Schedule A limits what transfers to RootWell-specific assets only.
- **Aniroop is correctly scoped.** RootWell TX only, no cross-entity bleed, 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff.

---

### Vulnerabilities and Open Issues

**1. Royalty rate is still TBD — this is a real risk**
The royalty license defers the rate to a financing/profitability threshold. That is directionally correct, but "TBD" is not enforceable. Before signing, a specific formula is required — e.g. 0% until $500K ARR or Series A close, then 3% of net revenue. The trigger must be drafted precisely by counsel or the royalty agreement is hollow.

**2. Joel's percentage interest is blank**
Both the Holdco Operating Agreement and related docs carry `[___]%` for Class B economic interest. This must be negotiated and documented before signing. An undocumented percentage is an open invitation to a future dispute.

**3. RecoverMargins / Delta 72 LLC relationship must be locked down**
RecoverMargins replaces the prior TradingLab concept. The key question is whether Delta 72 LLC owns all or part of RecoverMargins. The chosen path must be documented before any formation documents are drafted. No formation document should exist that assumes a structure not yet decided. Additionally, confirm in writing that Delta 72 LLC has no ownership of Coherence Labs IP LLC or RootWell & Star.

**4. Inventorship on the patent applications**
The provisional applications name Allison as sole inventor. If Joel contributed meaningfully to the technical architecture of the Coherence Score algorithm, he may have a legal inventorship claim under 35 U.S.C. § 116. This cannot be resolved by agreement — it is a factual legal determination. A patent attorney must assess the contribution records before filing.

**5. Pre-formation IP timeline gap**
All IP was created before either entity exists. The retroactive assignment addresses this legally, but the contribution ledger must establish clear dates and capacity (personal time vs. any employer obligations) for each item. If Allison or Joel held employment agreements with IP assignment clauses at the time of creation, those employers may have a prior claim.

**6. Phantom equity for Ric and David — completely undefined**
This remains undocumented. If either has been told verbally that they will receive something, that is an oral promise that creates legal exposure. Either draft the phantom equity agreements or document in writing that no commitment has been made.

**7. Aniroop's deliverables are still vague**
The restricted membership agreement has vesting mechanics but the deliverables tied to the cliff are bracketed placeholders. A specific scope-of-work exhibit must be attached before Aniroop signs anything.

**8. No operating bank accounts, EINs, or registered agent yet**
Nothing is legally real until the entities are filed, the EINs are obtained, and the documents are executed with counsel. All generated documents are drafts only — none have legal effect until that process is complete.
