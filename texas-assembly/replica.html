Educationals

Authentic American History

The government held in abeyance

The original federal government has been "held in abeyance" since 1868, warehoused by British/Scottish sympathizers in the Territorial Congress. One-third of the Federal Government is "missing." Reconstruction, begun in 1865, was never completed — the 50 State Assemblies exist to finish it.

⚖ What the law actually says

The federal government has operated continuously since 1789; no court recognizes any "abeyance." Reconstruction ended politically by 1877, and the Guarantee Clause commits republican-government questions to Congress — not private bodies. White v. Hart (1871); Luther v. Borden (1849).

The substitution of the States

After the Civil War the original "States of States" (e.g. "The State of Texas") were covertly replaced by British Territorial entities (e.g. "the State of Texas"). The "The/the" distinction is treated as legally operative.

⚖ What the law actually says

No court recognizes a legal distinction based on article capitalization. Texas was readmitted March 30, 1870, and the Supreme Court affirmed the continuity of state government. Texas v. White, 74 U.S. 700 (1869).

Land vs. sea jurisdiction & your political status

Two parallel systems exist — land/soil (living people, common law) and sea/admiralty (corporations, the current courts). Most Americans have been "misidentified" as Federal Citizens instead of American State Nationals, and the two statuses are mutually exclusive.

⚖ What the law actually says

Federal courts sit under Article III, not a separate admiralty system; admiralty jurisdiction (28 U.S.C. § 1333) is for maritime cases only. U.S. citizenship arises automatically at birth (14th Amendment; 8 U.S.C. § 1401), with no mechanism to "misidentify" it and no parallel exempt status. Dual federal/state citizenship is the default — there is no mutual exclusivity.

⚖ What the law actually says

Filing a "declaration of political status" with a notary or private recorder does not change your citizenship. The IRS, SSA, DMVs, and courts are not bound by it. A notary attests only to a signature, not to a document's truth or legal effect. Actual renunciation of U.S. citizenship requires a specific statutory procedure (8 U.S.C. § 1481); filing fraudulent documents with a county recorder can itself be a crime (Tex. Penal Code §§ 32.48, 32.49).

Treasury

Banking for State Nationals

The Global Family Group is creating accounts "specifically for American State Citizens and American State Nationals": a National Banking Account, an International Trade Account, and 5,000 AFD in automatic monthly deposits. AFD is defined as 1/10th oz of gold — "pre-paid credit, lawful currency, asset-backed 100% indemnified." You must be "already published" to open accounts.

⚖ What the law actually says

The Global Family Group is not a chartered bank (12 U.S.C. § 21 et seq.), not regulated by the OCC/FDIC or any state authority, and not in the FDIC database — deposits are not insured. Issuing an "AFD" instrument and promising automatic deposits raises issues under fictitious-obligations law (18 U.S.C. § 514), mail/wire fraud (§§ 1341, 1343), and state money-transmission licensing (Tex. Fin. Code § 151.301). "100% indemnified" is marketing, not a regulated guarantee.

Peacekeeping & Governance

Peacekeeping Task Force & Sheriffs

The Peacekeeping Task Force (PKTF) is a parallel structure to law enforcement, issuing a "Declaration of Peace" and an "International Peace Proclamation." The Assembly populates each county with a "Soil Jurisdiction Sheriff" — prioritizing counties within 100 air miles of international borders — and claims to fill "vacant Public Offices" dormant since the Civil War, citing the federal "100-mile Constitution-Free Zone."

⚖ What the law actually says

Private organizations cannot exercise law-enforcement authority. Peace officers derive authority from statute (Tex. Code Crim. Proc. Art. 2.12); Texas sheriffs are elected under the Texas Constitution and Local Gov't Code § 85.001. A privately chosen "sheriff" or task force has no arrest/search power, and claiming such office risks impersonating a public servant (Tex. Penal Code § 37.11). The real 100-mile CBP policy does not create a basis for private parallel enforcement.
