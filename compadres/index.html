01The full path

You deposit USD, it becomes a stable test-dollar (mUSDC), you buy CPDR at a fixed 1:1, route it to another compadre, and it can loop back out through a swap and an off-ramp — or exit a second way by burning to redeem. Click any node to see exactly what happens and what it costs.

02What's actually happening

The design rule is low extraction: every cost is a visible network fee, never a hidden spread, opaque venue cut, or an unlimited approval. Inside Compadres the only protocol fee on the whole loop is 5 bps on the toy AMM swap. The primary buy is 0 bps, the routed peer transfer is 0 bps (it only adds a clientOrderId tag so it can be reconciled), and burning is free. Everything else is just L2 gas, which on Base is a fraction of a cent.

04Extraction vs. transaction size

Flat fees (wire, gas) dominate small transfers and vanish on large ones; percentage fees (Stripe) stay constant. Compadres sits near the floor at every size because its only fee is a tiny basis-point swap charge. Both axes are logarithmic.

y = total cost as a % of the amount moved. Lower is better. Compadres lines hug the bottom across the whole $1–$10,000 range.

05Other dimensions that aren't price

Cheap is not the whole story. The same loop differs on finality, who holds your money, and how verifiable the record is. This is where the honey-backed version earns its keep: same low-extraction pipes, but the burn now redeems a real, inventoried good with signed reserve snapshots.
