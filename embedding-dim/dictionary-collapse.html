The Dictionary Collapse

65+ legal terms from Black's Law Dictionary embedded at 768 dimensions, then PCA-compressed to 16. Which distinctions survive?

The Dimensionality Illusion / Dictionary Collapse

The Experiment

We took terms from Black's Law Dictionary and cross-jurisdictional legal terminology — terms that carry different legal consequences despite surface similarity — and embedded them using nomic-embed-text (768 dimensions). Then we applied PCA at multiple compression levels and measured pairwise cosine similarity.

The question: at what dimensionality do legally distinct terms become indistinguishable to the embedding?

Term Groups

1. Custody & Parental Rights

These terms produce radically different legal outcomes but describe overlapping concepts. "Custody" implies a right held. "Allocation" implies a right distributed. "Guardianship" implies protective authority. In Illinois, the shift from "custody" to "allocation" in 2016 inverted the burden of proof.

What We Expect

Based on the theoretical framework:

  • Intra-domain pairs (custody/allocation, Pflichtteil/elective share, civil/criminal contempt) will see similarity increase by 0.15-0.30 at 16 dims — terms that are distinct collapse together.
  • Inter-domain pairs (custody/patent infringement) will see minimal change — they're already far apart in the principal components.
  • Cross-jurisdictional terms (Pflichtteil/laglott/iryubun) — terms from different legal traditions describing the same concept — will collapse into a single point, losing the tradition-specific distinctions.
  • Opposite pairs (discharge/nondischargeable, testamentary freedom/forced heirship) — terms with opposite legal meanings — may become more similar under PCA because they share the same domain vocabulary.
The punchline: At 16 dimensions, your retrieval system can tell law from medicine. But it can't tell "you go free" from "you go to jail." That's not compression. That's destruction.
