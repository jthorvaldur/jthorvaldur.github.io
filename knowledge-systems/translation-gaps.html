Paper 3 · Structural Epistemology

The words that don't exist shape
the science that can't emerge

When a concept has no English word, it creates a structural hole in the embedding space. That hole propagates through retrieval systems, citation networks, and funding decisions until the concept becomes invisible to Western science. This page maps those gaps.
