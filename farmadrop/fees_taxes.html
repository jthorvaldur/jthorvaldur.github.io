Section 1

The fee schedule — versioned, snapshotted, admin-editable

Rates are not constants in code. They live in a fee_schedules table; the in-force row is the one with the greatest effective_from ≤ now(). Every order snapshots the rates it was priced under onto its own row, so a future rate change can never reinterpret a past order. The admin dashboard can create future-dated schedules and may edit or delete only rows whose effective_from is still in the future — the temporal gate is enforced in SQL, not in the UI.

Section 2

One order, end to end — to the cent

All money arithmetic happens in one SQL function, compute_order_financials, surfaced as the order_financials view. App code never does money math. Every split is rounded to the cent and the platform takes the rounding residual — so the books always conserve: farmer + host + platform + tax = everything the member paid.

Transfer reserves (held, then realized)

0.25% of each payee's payout parks in Holding at receive and is realized to the platform at payout — it pre-funds the Express payout fee (§4). Nothing is credited early.

Section 3

The carve, not the surcharge

The take-rate page assumed the 2.9% card fee would be passed through to the member as a cart surcharge. The build decided otherwise — and it's the single most member-friendly call in the money path.

netBase = subtotal ÷ 1.029 — everyone absorbs Stripe proportionally.
The 2.9% is extracted from inside the subtotal before splitting, not added on top. The farmer's 80%, the host's 10% and the platform's 10% are all percentages of this slightly-smaller netBase, so all three parties share the card cost pro-rata. The member's only visible fee is the flat $0.55. What the map called a “3.15% cart surcharge” does not exist in the build.

Who actually pays Stripe

The platform's slice is the residual plug: grand total − tax − farmer − host. Out of it the platform pays Stripe's real processing cost (2.9% of the full charge + $0.30) — so the platform, not the farmer, wears the difference between the carve and Stripe's true bill.

Section 4

Lifecycle & the one reconcile loop

Money moves in stages, and each stage posts balanced ledger entries. Every external Stripe call is fronted by a durable outbox row (stripe_transactions) committed before the call, with an idempotency key — so a crash mid-call is recoverable and a retry can never double-charge.

Production: one EventBridge rate(5 minutes) → Lambda. Local dev: one setInterval. The web tier starts no timers at all — a deliberate rule so there is exactly one schedule to reason about.

Section 5

Refunds & unwinds — the paths that hurt

The ledger-structure page predicted the unwind paths would be the hard part. It was right. Here is what shipped and what is still queued.

Full cancel — shipped ✓

Member refund100% incl. the $0.55
Taxreversed at the original rate via Stripe Tax
Party earningsproportional reversal entries
Transfer reserveclawed back from Holding if not yet drained
Idempotencyoutbox row + retry loop (§4 jobs 3–5)

Conservation proven pre-payout, post-handover and post-payout in three dedicated test suites — the post-payout case books a clawback receivable exactly as the simulator page modeled.

Partial refund — queued 🟨

The math is shipped (compute_refund(order, fraction) prorates every percentage fee and keeps the fixed fee), but the DropSpot issue-resolution flow does not yet call Stripe, reverse tax, or post ledger entries for partial amounts. Blocked behind a ledger migration (work item CL → D2).

Until then, partial resolutions are operationally handled as needs-resolution placeholders — visible, not lost, just not yet money-moving.

Section 6

Open gaps — honest ledger of what's not done

The pending-fee matcher does not exist yet.
At payout the expected Express fee is accrued pending, stamped with the transfer id (tr_…). The balance sweep later books the real fee, stamped with the balance-transaction id (txn_…). No code links the two — pending accruals linger indefinitely. Documented, scoped as a follow-up, not started.
  • Partial refunds (D2). Wire refundOrder(fraction) into DropSpot issue resolution — Stripe call, tax reversal, ledger entries. Blocked by the ledger migration CL. next up
  • Outbox state-machine completion (E). The unknown / abandoned / backoff states and roll-backward path are deferred to a focused PR. deferred by decision
  • Express account monthly fee. Stripe may bill $2/mo per active Express account; not yet tracked in the ledger. minor
  • Tax authority sync. Authorities are seeded by migration; no dynamic sync from Stripe yet. minor
  • Multi-currency. Schema is ready, logic is single-USD. later
