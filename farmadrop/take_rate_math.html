Section 1

Why 0.10% cannot work — plainly

The take-% is carved out of gross basket volume and must simultaneously pay the contractor cut (3% of drop volume, §3.1), absorb the 2% credit boost (§3.4.2), and leave something for opex + growth. At 0.10% there is nothing to carve.

    Section 4

    Drop Score coupling — how ranking accelerates “double the farmer”

    The Drop Score (§5) is the discovery/ranking primitive. Coupling the take to it turns the fee schedule into a regenerative incentive: higher-scoring drops surface first (more volume) and pay a lower take (more kept). Two reinforcing levers push the farmer share up without raising member price.

    Lever A — discovery weighting

    Ranking is Drop-Score-weighted, so a high-score drop wins a disproportionate share of member demand. More throughput on the same fixed cost = lower effective take needed to hit the platform's net target.

    Lever B — score-decayed take (feeds T3)

    The take falls as a HostDrop's Drop-Score-weighted volume grows: bootstrap 12% → 7% at scale. Member-facing story: “fees fall as the network regenerates.” Farmer-facing story: “the better you farm, the more you keep.” This is the growth-capital argument — a high early take funds host-drop density & CAC (§3.4.6), then decays as the score-weighted network compounds.

    Guard-rail: the Drop Score must be a transparent guidance tree with gaming-resistance (§5) before the take is tied to it — otherwise the fee schedule is only as trustworthy as the score.
